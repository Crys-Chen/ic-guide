# MIT 18.700: Linear Algebra

## Descriptions

- Offered by: MIT
- Prerequisites: MIT 18.02 (multivariable calculus)
- Programming Languages: None
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟🌟🌟
- Estimated Hours: ~36 hours per semester

MIT's proof-based undergraduate linear algebra course, positioned as the "theory-heavy upgrade" of 18.06. Uses Sheldon Axler's *Linear Algebra Done Right*, starting from the axioms of abstract vector spaces and de-emphasizing mechanical matrix computation in favor of inner-product spaces, the spectral theorem, and Jordan normal form. Matches the abstraction level of the second semester of Chinese "advanced algebra" and serves as the best bridge from linear algebra to abstract algebra.

OCW provides full lecture notes, problem sets, and solutions, but **no video** (the biggest gap). Pair with Axler's free companion videos.
