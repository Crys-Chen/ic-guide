# MIT 6.875: Foundations of Cryptography

## Descriptions

- Offered by: MIT
- Prerequisites: Abstract algebra, probability, basic complexity theory
- Programming Languages: None
- Instructors: Shafi Goldwasser (Turing Award), Vinod Vaikuntanathan
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟
- Estimated Hours: ~36 hours

The most authoritative course on theoretical cryptography, co-taught by Turing-laureate Shafi Goldwasser and Vinod Vaikuntanathan. Starts from Shannon's information-theoretic limit of the one-time pad and works through pseudorandom generators, zero-knowledge proofs, secure multi-party computation (MPC), and fully homomorphic encryption — a graduate-level foundation of cryptographic theory.

Pairs with Stanford Boneh's course as the "applied vs theoretical" complement: Boneh teaches "how to use", Goldwasser teaches "why this is secure".
