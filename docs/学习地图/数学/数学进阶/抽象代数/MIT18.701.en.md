# MIT 18.701/18.702: Algebra I/II (Artin)

## Descriptions

- Offered by: MIT
- Prerequisites: MIT 18.700 (proof-based linear algebra)
- Programming Languages: None
- Instructor: Michael Artin
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟
- Estimated Hours: ~80 hours over two semesters

MIT's honors abstract-algebra sequence taught by algebraic geometer Michael Artin (son of Emil Artin). Distinctive **geometric entry point** — groups introduced via matrix groups, symmetries, and lattice groups rather than the traditional permutation-first approach. Uses Artin's own textbook *Algebra* (also used at Harvard Math 122), widely regarded as the most taste-defining abstract algebra textbook.

OCW provides lecture notes and assignments but **no video** — though Artin's notes are self-sufficient.
